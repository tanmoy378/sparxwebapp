This table is created for calculating delta survey date, delta Asset Remaining Life and Asset degradation rate for a given site, asset and survey. All these measures subsequently get used in calculating the predicted year of expiry for a given asset for a given site and survey<br/>

ASSET_REMAINING_LIFE Public int
Notes: ASSET_REMAINING_LIFE holds the remaining life of the asset as a numerical value (in years)<br/>
PERFORMANCE Public int
Notes: This is also called the ‘F3’ factor value and is entered by surveyor at the time of condition surveying of an asset as an integer from 1 to 5. This relates to the effect on people moving around the site. Remember - This is a best professional opinion at the time of survey if the asset failed.<br/>• 1 = A restriction on movement within a staff only area. <br/>• 2 = A restriction on movement within a public area.<br/>• 3 = A partial closure of the site would be required. <br/>• 4 = A TSR / PSR may be needed<br/>• 5 = The line or site may need to be closed. <br/><br/>
SAFETY Public int
Notes: This is also called the F4 factor value and is entered by surveyor at the time of condition surveying of an asset as an integer from 1 to 5 This is the effect of asset failure on the safety of people using the site, according to best professional judgement.<br/>• 1 = a single minor injury could occur<br/>• 2 = multiple minor injuries could occur<br/>• 3 = a RIDDOR reportable injury could occur<br/>• 4 = a single serious injury could occur<br/>• 5 = a single fatality or multiple serious injury could occur.<br/>
PROBABILITY Public int
Notes: This is also called the F5 factor value and is entered by surveyor at the time of condition surveying of an asset as an integer from 1 to 5. This is the frequency of the hazard occurring at the site ;<br/>• 1 = Less than 1 in 25 years<br/>• 2 = Less than 1 in 5 years to 1 in 25 years<br/>• 3 = Less than 1 per year to 1 in 5 years <br/>• 4 = 1 to 5 incidents per year<br/>• 5 = 5 incidents per year or more<br/>
FABRIC Public int
Notes: This is also called the F6 factor value and is entered by surveyor at the time of condition surveying of an asset as an integer from 1 to 5<br/>– This factor value looks at how asset failure will affect the structure it is part of;<br/>– 1 = Isolated defects will allow further deterioration<br/>– 2 = Major isolated or a series of defects will affect whole structure<br/>– 3 = Minor collapse will occur of there will be a failure of elements<br/>– 4 = Major failure will occur which affects the whole structure<br/>– 5 = Entire structure collapses.<br/>
QUANTITY Public int
Notes: This is the number of units of asset<br/>
RISK_SCORE Public int
Notes: RISK_SCORE specifies the asset risk score as a numerical value. This is calculated as: Asset Risk Score = Log<sub>5 </sub>(5^F3 + 5^F4). Where F3, F4 are the Performance and Safety Factor values respectively<br/>
ASSET_REMAINING_LIFE_PERCENT Public int
Notes: ASSET_REMAINING_LIFE_PERCENTAGE is a numeric value which specify the asset remaining life in %<br/>Asset remaining life Percentage = <br/>100* (Asset Remaining Life in Years/ Asset Life Expectancy in Years)<br/>
CONDITION_WEIGHTING Public int
Notes: This is also called Asset Weighting Factor (AWF). It is aa rating on a scale of 1 to 5 assigned to each asset within the measure where:<br/><br/>1  insignificant item - minimal impact on the measure, if renewed<br/><br/>5  very significant item - may have immediate impact on a station and<br/>would have longer term liabilities<br/><br/>The AWF is designed to reflect the relative importance of each asset on the basis of providing safe and efficient operational use of the station.<br/>
CONDITION_RATING_WEIGHTING Public int
Notes: This is calculated by multiplying the Asset Condition Rating with Condition Weighting<br/><br/>Condition Weighting Rating = Asset Condition Rating * Condition Weighting<br/><br/>Asset Condition Rating is a rating band based on an index of 1 to 5, with 1 representing ‘as installed’ and 5 as no longer serviceable or life expired, based on the PARL.The bands are defined as:<br/><br/>PARL        Band<br/>#lt;1               5<br/>1 – 15        4<br/>16 – 45       3<br/>46 – 75       2<br/>#gt; = 76        1<br/>
SURVEY_DAYS_DIFF Public int
Notes: This is the difference of survey date with previous survey date (in number of days) calculated for a given asset id by first ordering the dataset by asset id and survey date in ascending order. Calculation mechanism is:<br/><br/>Step 1: Select Asset Id, Element Id, Feature Id, Survey Date, Residual Life (calculated column : (Factor 1 * 10 + Factor 2)) from Surveys data (order the data by Asset Id and Survey Date)<br/><br/>STEP 2: Compare the current row with the previous row and calculate the Delta Survey Date in days i.e., Current Row - Previous Row for an Asset Id<br/>
DELTA_ASSET_REMAINING_LIFE Public int
Notes: This is the difference of ARL(Residual Life or Remaining Life) calculated in current survey with ARL calculated in previous survey for a given asset id by first ordering the dataset by asset id and survey date in ascending order. Calculation mechanism is:<br/><br/>Step 1: Select Asset Id, Element Id, Feature Id, Survey Date, Residual Life (calculated column : (Factor 1 * 10 + Factor 2)) from Surveys data (order the data by Asset Id and Survey Date)<br/><br/>STEP 2: Compare the current row with the previous row and calculate the Delta ARL in years i.e., Current Row - Previous Row for an Asset Id<br/>
DEGRADATION_RATE Public numeric(10,2)
Notes: Rate of asset degradation - This is calculated by dividing Delta ARL (in days) by Delta Survey Date. If the delta survey date is zero for a record, the asset degradation rate is assigned a value of 0<br/>
